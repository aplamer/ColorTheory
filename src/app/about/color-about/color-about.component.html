Color

Color is the property which gives an object a specific appearance based off of how the object absorbs and reflects light. Though color may be a concept that many of us would consider ourselves familiar with, how it actually works and the entire spectrum of it is something most don't understand.

Color is composed of 3 major properties: Hue, Saturation, and Value.

If you've ever worked with a color picker then you've already seen how all three of these properties interact with one another to create a color.

To put it simply you can imagine Saturation and Value each as a number from 0 to 100 and Hue as a number from 0 to 360. How you get a specific color is by combining differing intensities of all three. Even changing one of these properties can have a drastic effect on how a color can change.

Saturation should be in higher amounts when you want to draw attention to a specific thing in a design or when you want to capture a dream like, abstract or cartoony nature and a lower amount when you want to ground objects in reality or make it more digestible. Value should be used to group objects together or seperate objects from one another in order to create contrast or unity, colors of similar values will look like they belong with one another and colors with very different values will feel seperate from one another. Hue is special in that using it effectively relies so heavily on the other parts of color theory (Symbolism and Harmony), and it doesn't really do much on it's own like Value and Saturation do.
